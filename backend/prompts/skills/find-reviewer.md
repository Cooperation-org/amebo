For our products, one credible person actually trying it and writing about it is
usually worth more than a cold sequence. This skill finds that person and sets up
the ask. The goal is a genuine trial plus honest feedback — not a paid plug.

## What to do
1. **Anchor on the archetype + the product's real hook** (from `product-gtm` or
   the experiment doc): who would find this genuinely interesting to try, and why
   would *their* audience care.
2. **Build a reviewer profile, not just a name** — someone credible to the
   archetype who writes/posts where our customers already are (newsletter, blog,
   podcast, Bluesky/LinkedIn, a specific community). Look for fits we already know
   (abra, CRM) before going outward; for new ones, give the human concrete
   criteria — amebo can't open-web search, so don't fabricate names.
3. **Make the ask honest and low-friction:** we want them to actually try it and
   say what they really think, good or bad. Offer to do the setup, sit for a short
   interview, or co-write (load `content-from-interview`) so it's easy and in their
   own voice — never ghostwrite a fake endorsement.
4. **Turn it into motion:** draft the outreach and the follow-through as gated
   Taiga tasks (due + cash), and plan how we capture and act on their feedback.

## Output
The reviewer profile, candidate fits from our network + criteria for more, the
drafted ask, and the gated next-step task(s).

## Don't
- Don't buy or fake endorsements; honest feedback (including negative) is the point.
- Don't pick someone whose audience isn't the archetype.
- Tasks gated and dated; the ask is for a real trial, not a plug.
