name: clarify-task
description: When a task handed to amebo is vague, one-line, or underdefined, pin down the deliverable and definition-of-done BEFORE doing work. Use whenever the ask is ambiguous enough that you could plausibly build the wrong thing.
---

## When to use
A task arrives underspecified — "look into X", "help with the deck", "sort out the CRM" — and there's real risk of delivering the wrong thing, or the outcome / owner / deadline isn't stated.

## What to do
Ask the fewest questions that remove the real ambiguity, then reflect back a crisp definition-of-done for the human to confirm.

- Ask only what changes what you'd do — 1–3 sharp questions, never a questionnaire. If a sensible default exists, state it and let them correct it rather than ask.
- Nail the **deliverable and done-criteria** ("what does good look like, and how will we know it's done?"), plus owner and any deadline, before scope drifts.
- Check **abra first** for existing context (past notes, related project) so you don't ask what's already known.
- If the human is senior and moving fast, offer one proposed interpretation to approve instead of open questions.

## Output
A one-line restatement of the task + its definition-of-done, plus only the genuinely blocking questions. Then wait for confirmation before doing the work.

## Don't
- Don't guess silently and run — a wrong deliverable costs more than a question.
- Don't dump a long list of questions; ambiguity doesn't mean ask everything.
- Don't ask what a sensible default or a quick abra lookup already answers.
