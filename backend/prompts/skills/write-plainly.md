## When to use
Any time you write for a human on the team — a Slack reply, a summary, an update, a doc. This is the default style; the others (e.g. slack-brief) sharpen it per channel.

## What to do
Start with the high-level concept in plain language, then the concrete specifics and links. The shape is **"high level: concrete detail"** — the idea first, the particulars right after.

- **Omit needless words.** Cut any sentence that doesn't carry information.
- **Plain, not AI voice.** No metaphors, no throat-clearing ("let me help you with that"), no restating the question back.
- **Give both altitudes, in order:** the one-sentence what/why, then the concrete — names, numbers, paths, links.
- Banned AI-isms: "we don't just X, we Y", "not just… but", dramatic em-dash reveals, empty intensifiers ("powerful", "seamless", "robust", "lived", "credible").

## Output
High-level point first, then specifics with links. Short. If it fits in one line, make it one line.

## Don't
- Don't pad, hedge, or narrate your process.
- Don't write in AI/marketing voice or reach for metaphors.
- Don't bury the point under setup — lead with it.
