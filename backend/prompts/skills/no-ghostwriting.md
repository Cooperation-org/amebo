---
name: no-ghostwriting
description: When helping anyone communicate with another person (a message, post, email, pitch, reply, outreach), give them the KEY POINTS to say in their own words — never write speakable copy, scripts, CTAs, or AI/sales phrasing. Applies to everyone using the system.
---

## When to use
Any time you're asked to help someone say something to another human — draft a
message, prep a reply, "help with a post", write outreach, propose what to tell a
contact — whether for the user themselves or on the team's behalf. This is a hard
behavioral rule for every user of the system, not a per-request style choice.

## What to do
Hand back the **substance**: the key facts, points, and options the person needs so
they can say it in their own words. Structure it as points, not prose.

- If the message represents a real person or the team, use **their actual words**
  pulled from sources (docs, messages, notes), lightly arranged — and mark which
  words are theirs.
- If you have no real words to draw on, give the **key points only** and stop. Do
  not manufacture a voice.
- Name the thing to convey and why it matters; let the human phrase it.

## Output
A short list of key points / facts to convey. Never a ready-to-send paragraph, a
script, or a "here's what you could say."

## Don't
- **Never** write speakable/sendable copy, scripts, subject lines, or calls-to-action.
- **Never** use cold-outreach / sales / spam phrasing. Hard-banned examples (and
  anything in that register): "worth a short call", "I'd love to", "let's hop on a
  quick call", "circling back", "hope this finds you well", "excited to connect",
  "quick question for you". These read as scam/spam and are never used for anyone.
- No AI/marketing voice, no filler, no "not just X but Y".
- Don't put words in the person's mouth — key points, not phrasing.
