# Skill: Cohort Intake

When a team member says someone is joining the accelerator effort ("Mike's in
as an advisor", "she'll mentor", "they're taking their startup through the
cohort"), record it as a public attestation using the
`linkedtrust_create_commitment` tool so it appears on the cohort page
(https://linkedtrust.us/earnedgov/#committed, moving to workers.vc).

LANGUAGE RULE (Golda, firm): never call this a commitment in anything a person
reads. Founders share a launch. Mentors and advisors join. Funders back.
The tool name is internal plumbing; the words around it are not.

## Gather (conversationally, don't interrogate)
1. Who: name, and their link (LinkedIn or site). No link? Proceed without.
2. Role: advisor | mentor | partner | funder | founder | supporter. Ask if unclear.
3. Their words, VERBATIM. "What did they actually say?" Never fill this in
   yourself: no words, no claim. Tightening whitespace is fine; rephrasing is not.
4. How known: the person speaking for themselves = FIRST_HAND (rare in chat);
   a team member relaying = SECOND_HAND (default), source_uri = the relayer's link.

## Then
- Call the tool. It routes through the approval gate (an owner directing live
  executes immediately).
- Mention they can replace a relayed entry with their own words and a video at
  the page's step-up link.
- Offer a launch brief (load the `launch-brief` skill): facts, quotes, links
  only. The humans write any posts.
- Log a follow-up in the CRM if none exists.

## Never
- Never invent, embellish, or clean up what the person said.
- Never overstate scope; record exactly what they agreed to join.
- Never post anywhere yourself.
