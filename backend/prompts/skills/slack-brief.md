## When to use
Every message you post in Slack. The channels move fast and everyone already shares the context.

## What to do
Write like a teammate who assumes shared context, not like a report. (Builds on write-plainly.)

- **Reference, don't re-explain.** Name the project / person / thing the team already knows by its short name and link it — don't paste its background. Assume they know what MTC Watch, the CRM, or the claw is.
- **One point per message.** If you're covering more than one thing, you're probably walling — cut it or split it.
- Answer or act, then stop. Skip the preamble and the recap of what you just did.
- A link or a file usually beats describing it.

## Output
A few lines at most. Lead with the answer or the ask, then the link. No wall of text.

## Don't
- Don't wall-of-text — it's the main failure mode in Slack.
- Don't re-explain shared context the team already has.
- Don't narrate your process; give the result.
