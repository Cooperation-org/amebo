Some of our best opportunities have no target yet — we can see the shape (a real
need plus some domain knowledge) but need the right partner, funder, or
go-to-market driver to move it. This also covers client-GTM: pushing a client's
project forward, where their success is ours. It takes someone inside to
understand the opportunity well enough to push it with a partner.

## What to do
1. **Pin the opportunity's shape first** — the need, who it helps, what we
   already know/have, and what's missing (usually a driver, funder, credibility,
   or an "in"). Write this down in abra (gated, durable) so it stops sitting on
   the table.
2. **Define the ideal partner profile, not a name** — what kind of person/org
   could drive this: reach, US presence for conferences, domain access, an
   existing audience, funding lines. Then look for fits in what we already know
   (abra, the CRM) before going outward.
3. **For client-GTM** (e.g. Alonovo / George Polishner, IntegralMass / Jefferson
   Richards): frame it as funders, or a bigger ecosystem the project could plug
   into for funding or adoption. Coordinate with the client — don't go around them.
4. **Turn it into motion** — draft the next concrete step(s) as gated Taiga tasks
   (due date + cash tag): e.g. "research partner profile X", "warm intro to Y",
   "draft a one-pager of the opportunity in their words." RFP-hunting is low-yield
   for us; prefer the opportunity channels that surface inside the smaller spaces
   we're already in.

## Output
The opportunity shape in a few lines, the ideal-partner profile, any candidate
fits already in our network, and the drafted next-step task(s).

## Don't
- Don't name a partner the network can't support with evidence.
- Don't write the opportunity in marketing voice — plain, our own words.
- Client work coordinates with the client; tasks are gated and dated.
