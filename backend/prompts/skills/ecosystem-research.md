Cold email alone does not work for us — the win is participating in the smaller
spaces a target already cares about. This skill fills in that picture so a human
can engage there usefully and on the target's own terms.

## What to do
1. **Pull what we already know first** — abra and the CRM for the contact, their
   org, prior threads, and any channels already on file; Slack history if we've
   talked about them. Don't re-research what's already recorded.
2. **Build the target's "where + what".** *Where:* the spaces they're active in
   (Discord, Slack, Signal group, mailing list, forum, ATProto/Bluesky, GitHub).
   *What* (more important): what they are trying to make happen — a tech they
   want adopted, an ecosystem they're growing, a problem they're pushing on. The
   point is to be genuinely useful in their terms, not to pitch.
3. **Be honest about reach.** Amebo can read a known public URL but has no
   open-web search. If a space isn't discoverable from what we already have, say
   what the human should go look up — never fabricate a handle, channel, or link.
4. **Don't lose the findings.** Record them so the next person benefits: durable
   "who they are / what they want" into abra; channel facts belong with the
   contact in the CRM. (Recording to the CRM and reading arbitrary web pages need
   `odoo_cli` and `http_fetch` enabled for this instance — if they aren't, hand
   the findings back for a human to file and say so.)

## Output
A short profile: spaces they're in (with links/handles where known), what they're
trying to do, and 1-2 concrete ways to show up there usefully. Flag unknowns plainly.

## Don't
- Don't invent handles, channels, or links.
- Don't recommend cold mass email as the move.
- Read-mostly — any CRM/abra write is a gated draft, never silent.
