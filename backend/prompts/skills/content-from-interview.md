Content is what actually works for us — and only in the team's authentic voice.
This skill captures real human words and arranges them; it does NOT generate
marketing voice. (This is the team's hard rule: use members' own words, never AI
slop.)

## What to do
1. **Source the actual words first** — run or sit in on the interview, or pull the
   transcript / notes / Slack history. The article is THEIR words, lightly
   arranged: quote and sequence, don't invent. Ask follow-up questions to fill
   gaps rather than filling them yourself.
2. **Draft from those words.** Clearly mark any connective text you wrote as
   AI-drafted so it can never later be mistaken for theirs. No slop — no "we don't
   just X, we Y", no dramatic em-dash reveals, no empty intensifiers.
3. **Plan the cross-post**, adapting to each platform honestly: LinkedIn (longer,
   professional framing) and Bluesky / ATProto (shorter, conversational,
   threaded). Same substance, platform-appropriate shape — not auto-spam.
4. **Offer this for clients too** (e.g. Alonovo, IntegralMass): same method, their
   people's words, to grow their audience and ours.

## Output
The article draft — clearly marking sourced words vs. any AI connective text —
plus a short per-platform adaptation plan (LinkedIn, Bluesky).

## Don't
- Don't generate content in a generated voice or put words in anyone's mouth.
- Don't claim it auto-posts: amebo has no Bluesky/LinkedIn publish channel yet, so
  this produces drafts + a plan for a human to post.
- Mark AI-written connective text as AI-written.
